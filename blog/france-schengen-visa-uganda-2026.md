# How to Apply for a France Schengen Visa from Uganda (2026)

France is the most visited country in the world and one of the most requested Schengen destinations for Ugandan travelers. Whether you are going for tourism, business, or to visit family, this guide walks you through the exact steps to apply for your France Schengen visa from Kampala.

## Overview

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Visa type | Short-stay Schengen (Type C) |
| Maximum stay | 90 days within 180 days |
| Consulate fee | €90 (non-refundable) |
| Processing time | 15–21 business days |
| Where to apply | VFS Global Kampala |
| Earliest application | 6 months before travel |
| Latest application | At least 3 weeks before travel |

## Step 1 — Determine the Purpose of Your Visit

France issues different visa categories depending on your purpose:

- **Tourism (C-Visit)** — sightseeing, visiting friends or family
- **Business (C-Business)** — meetings, conferences, trade fairs
- **Transit (Airport Transit)** — passing through a French airport

Be honest about your purpose. Applying for a tourism visa when your actual purpose is business is a common mistake that leads to refusal and complicates future applications.

## Step 2 — Gather Your Documents

**Mandatory for all applicants:**
- Valid Ugandan passport (minimum 3 months validity beyond return date, 2 blank pages)
- Two passport photos (white background, 35mm x 45mm, taken within the last 6 months)
- Completed Schengen application form (available at VFS Global or online)
- Travel insurance — minimum €30,000 coverage, must cover repatriation, valid for entire Schengen area

**Financial documents:**
- Personal bank statements for the last 3 months
- Minimum balance should reflect approximately €65 per day of your stay
- If employed: recent payslips (last 3 months)
- If self-employed: business registration certificate and bank statements

**Travel arrangements:**
- Return flight booking confirmation
- Hotel reservations or accommodation proof for every night

**Employment or study proof:**
- Employed: employer letter confirming position, salary, leave approval, and that you will return
- Self-employed: business registration documents
- Student: school letter confirming enrolment and leave

**For business travel, additionally:**
- Invitation letter from the French company or organisation
- Business registration documents

## Step 3 — Book Your VFS Appointment

All France Schengen visa applications in Uganda go through **VFS Global Kampala** on Yusuf Lule Road, Nakasero.

1. Go to vfsglobal.com and select France as your destination country
2. Choose Uganda as your location
3. Book an appointment — slots are available approximately 2–3 weeks in advance
4. During peak season (June–August), book 6–8 weeks in advance

**Important:** Do not book flights or non-refundable hotels before you have your VFS appointment confirmed. If you cannot get an appointment in time, your travel plans may need to change.

## Step 4 — Attend Your Appointment

Arrive at VFS Global at least 15 minutes before your appointment. Bring:

- All original documents plus one photocopy of each
- Your appointment confirmation
- Payment for the consulate fee (€90) and VFS service charge (approximately UGX 100,000)

VFS will collect your biometrics (fingerprints and photo) if this is your first Schengen application or if your biometric data is more than 5 years old.

Your passport will be retained by VFS during processing. Do not make other travel plans that require your passport during this period.

## Step 5 — Wait for the Decision

Standard processing is **15–21 business days** from the date of your appointment. This does not include weekends or public holidays.

You will receive an SMS or email from VFS when your passport is ready for collection. Do not contact VFS before the stated processing time has elapsed.

The French consulate may request additional documents during processing. Respond promptly — delays in providing requested documents can result in refusal.

## Understanding Your Visa

If approved, your visa sticker will show:

- **Valid from / Until** — the dates you may be in the Schengen area
- **Duration of stay** — usually 90 days or the exact number of days you requested
- **Number of entries** — Single or Multiple. A multiple-entry visa allows you to enter and exit the Schengen area more than once within the validity period

Always enter the Schengen area through France if France is the main destination of your trip. Entering through another Schengen country when France is your primary destination is technically a violation and can cause issues at border control.

## Tips for a Strong Application

**Match your bank balance to your trip cost.** Calculate €65 per day and make sure your average balance over three months supports this. A sudden large deposit the week before you apply raises immediate red flags.

**Book refundable accommodation.** If your visa is refused, non-refundable hotel bookings are a painful and avoidable loss. Use Booking.com with free cancellation options.

**Write a detailed cover letter.** This is optional but strongly recommended. A clear, honest explanation of your trip purpose, itinerary, and ties to Uganda significantly strengthens your file. Ailes Global prepares this as part of our service.

**Apply for the exact dates you need.** Do not exaggerate your travel dates hoping for a longer visa. Request exactly what you need with clear supporting documents.

**Previous Schengen history helps.** If you have previously held a Schengen visa, include a copy of the relevant pages in your passport. A clean visa history substantially improves approval chances.
